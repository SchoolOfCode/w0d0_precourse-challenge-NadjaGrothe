Freddie Mercury

Lead singer of Queen and one of the greatest singers in the history of rock music.

A Timeline of Freddie Mercury's Life

  • Farrokh Bulsara was born on 5 September 1946 in Stone Town Zanzibar.
  • He spent the bulk of his childhood in India where he attended English-stype boarding schools. He began taking piano lessons at the age of seven.
  • In 1964 his family fled the Zanzibar Revolution and moved to Middlesex, England.
  • In 1970 he formed Queen with guitarist Brian May and drummer Roger Taylor and legally changed his name to Freddie Mercury. Bassist John Deacon joined Queen in 1971.
  • In 1973 the band released their debut album 'Queen'.
  • In 1985 Freddie Mercury released his first solo album 'Mr. Bad Guy'.
  • In 1988 his second and last solo album 'Barcelona' was released. 'Barcelona' was recorder in collaboration Spanish soprano vocalist Montserrat.
  • As a member of Queen, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of fame in 2001.
  • Freddie Mercury passed away on 24 November 1991 at the age of 45 due to bronchial pneumonia resulting from AIDS. He publicly announced his diagnosis a day before his death but had known about it since 1987.
